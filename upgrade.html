Olympia: The Funding Layer for Ethereum Classic

Olympia is the upgrade that gives Ethereum Classic a sustainable, protocol-native funding layer. It unifies modern fee mechanics, an immutable Treasury, and on-chain governance into a coherent, transparent system. Defined across ECIPs 1111 through 1115, Olympia introduces these capabilities without changing ETC’s fixed monetary policy, miner incentives, or Proof-of-Work consensus.

Olympia defines how BASEFEE accounting, a non-inflationary Treasury, and an on-chain governance and funding process work together at the protocol and contract layers. It is designed for the people who rely on the network—miners, developers, node operators, and ecosystem builders—by providing a transparent, accountable way to sustain clients, infrastructure, and public goods without depending on centralized donors or off-chain sponsors. As of November 11, 2025 (“Olympia Day”), Ethereum Classic core developers have moved from the ECIP drafting phase into reference implementation, integrating the consensus-layer components of ECIP-1111 into Fukuii, a modern, Scala-based ETC client derived from Mantis. Fukuii is maintained independently of the Ethereum Foundation’s Proof-of-Stake roadmap, giving Ethereum Classic a native, long-term client path for validating and deploying Olympia on testnets and, ultimately, mainnet.

The Olympia ECIP Suite

The Olympia ECIPs are designed to be modular but complementary. Each proposal focuses on one part of the system, and together they form a complete pathway from no protocol funding to a transparent, on-chain treasury and DAO.

ECIP-1111 Olympia EVM & Protocol Upgrades

Introduces EIP-1559-style fee mechanics and the BASEFEE opcode (EIP-3198) on Ethereum Classic. BASEFEE becomes a protocol-set minimum fee per unit of gas. On ETC, unlike Ethereum mainnet, 100% of BASEFEE is redirected at the consensus layer to the Olympia Treasury (ECIP-1112) instead of being burned.

  • Modernizes ETC’s fee market with EIP-1559-style accounting.
  • Improves compatibility with contemporary EVM tooling and wallets.
  • Preserves Proof-of-Work and fixed monetary policy.
  • Provides the plumbing for protocol-native, non-inflationary funding.

ECIP-1112 Olympia Treasury Contract

Defines a deterministic, immutable Treasury smart contract that receives all BASEFEE redirected by ECIP-1111. The Treasury is governance-isolated: it has no admin keys, no upgrade paths, and no minting functions. Funds may only be withdrawn through a single authorized execution entry point controlled by Olympia DAO.

  • No upgrade keys, backdoors, or mutable parameters.
  • Acts purely as a vault for consensus-directed BASEFEE revenue.
  • All deposits and withdrawals are transparent and auditable on-chain.

ECIP-1113 Olympia DAO Governance Framework

Specifies a modular DAO architecture that serves as the sole authorized path to the Treasury. Olympia DAO is built around a Governor, Timelock, and Executor design and launches without a governance token. Voting models and identity systems are introduced separately through the Olympia Improvement Proposal (OIP) process.

  • Defines the Governor → Timelock → Executor execution pipeline.
  • Supports Child-DAOs and specialized funding streams.
  • Does not hard-code voter identity or a specific sybil-resistance model.

ECIP-1114 Ethereum Classic Funding Proposal Process

Formalizes the Ethereum Classic Funding Proposal (ECFP) standard—the exclusive, hash-bound mechanism for spending from the Olympia Treasury. Each proposal is tied to immutable, content-addressed metadata and moves through a clear lifecycle from submission to execution.

  • Defines the canonical ECFP data model and proposal states.
  • Requires deterministic, hash-bound payloads for Treasury withdrawals.
  • Supports milestone-based payouts and works for both community and institutional proposals.

ECIP-1115 Olympia L-Curve Smoothing (Optional)

Introduces an optional governance-layer smoothing mechanism that applies a deterministic L-curve to Treasury-held BASEFEE. Smoothing shapes the timing of governance-approved payouts to miners, without changing their total amount or altering consensus rules.

  • Helps miners plan around variable fee revenue over longer time horizons.
  • Operates entirely at the contract and governance layer—no consensus changes required.
  • Can be enabled, tuned, or disabled through the OIP process as the network matures.

Layered Architecture: ECIPs 1111 and 1112 form the minimal consensus and Treasury foundation. ECIPs 1113 and 1114 add DAO governance and the ECFP funding pipeline. ECIP-1115 is optional, operating purely at the governance layer to smooth payouts over time.

Design Principles

Olympia is intentionally aligned with Ethereum Classic’s long-standing philosophy. Each ECIP is written to respect a small set of non-negotiable constraints:

  • No monetary inflation or new governance token required.
  • Proof-of-Work consensus and chain history are preserved.
  • Immutability and the Code is Law ethos remain intact.
  • Funding flows are transparent, on-chain, and auditable.
  • Governance is modular and upgradeable at the contract layer.
  • EVM compatibility and client diversity are treated as first-class goals.

Instead of introducing a new “governance coin” or routing decisions through a single foundation, Olympia:

  • Uses existing protocol revenue (BASEFEE) instead of new issuance.
  • Keeps the Treasury contract immutable while allowing governance modules to evolve.
  • Separates capabilities (what the protocol can do) from social choices (who participates and how).
  • Maintains EVM compatibility by adopting 1559-style accounting without burning.

Questions like “Who gets to vote?”, “How are delegates chosen?”, or “Which identity system should be used?” are deliberately pushed to the social layer and to future Olympia Improvement Proposals (OIPs), not enshrined in the base protocol.

Deployment Path & Phasing

Olympia follows the existing ECIP-1000 process, Ethereum Classic’s standard path for network upgrades. It is expected to roll out in phases, with careful testing and community review at each step. As of November 11, 2025 (“Olympia Day”), the project has moved from the ECIP drafting phase into reference implementation, with the consensus-layer changes of ECIP-1111 under active development in the Fukuii client.

  1. Research & Historical Context
    Years of ETC community calls and research documented long-term funding challenges, client fragility, and the need for a protocol-native, non-inflationary Treasury mechanism.
  2. ECIP Drafting
    ECIPs 1111–1115 are drafted as a tightly scoped, modular suite, each with a single, well-defined responsibility and clear interfaces to the others.
  3. Testnet Implementations
    Client and contract implementations are deployed on testnets (such as Mordor) to validate behavior, economic assumptions, and operator experience.
  4. Community Review & Refinement
    Miners, node operators, developers, and ecosystem participants review the behavior, discuss tradeoffs, and suggest refinements via ECIP discussions and open calls.
  5. Mainnet Activation (Pending Consensus)
    If broad consensus forms, Olympia is scheduled for a future network upgrade through the established ECIP-1000 governance process.
Specification-Driven Review: Decisions about Olympia are made through the ECIP-1000 process, with feedback grounded in the written specifications and observed behavior on testnets, rather than personal preference or off-chain influence.
