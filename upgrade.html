ECIPs 1111–1115 · Drafted as a Modular Suite

A Cohesive Upgrade for Fees, Treasury, and Governance

Olympia is a modular upgrade suite for Ethereum Classic that connects modern fee mechanics, a protocol-native treasury, and on-chain governance into one coherent system. It is composed of five ECIPs—1111 through 1115—that can be adopted together or in stages, without changing ETC’s fixed-emission schedule or Proof-of-Work consensus.

This page provides the high-level upgrade story. Individual subpages dive deeper into fees, the treasury contract, governance design, ecosystem impact, and long-term vision.

The Olympia ECIP Suite

The Olympia ECIPs are designed to be modular but complementary. Each proposal addresses a specific part of the system, and together they form a complete pathway from “no protocol funding” to a transparent, on-chain treasury and DAO.

ECIP–1111 Olympia EVM & Protocol Upgrades

Introduces EIP-1559-style BASEFEE accounting, type-2 transactions, and related EVM compatibility improvements. Instead of burning BASEFEE, ETC redirects it to a consensus-selected destination, enabling protocol-native funding without inflation.

  • Modernizes ETC’s fee market and transaction types.
  • Preserves Proof-of-Work and fixed monetary policy.
  • Provides the plumbing for BASEFEE redirection (e.g., to the Olympia Treasury).
Learn more in the Fee Mechanics section →

ECIP–1112 Olympia Treasury Contract

Defines a protocol-controlled, immutable treasury smart contract that can receive BASEFEE and disburse funds only via standardized governance calls.

  • No upgrade keys, no backdoors, no minting functions.
  • Designed purely as a vault for protocol revenue.
  • Auditable on-chain, with clear invariants and access controls.
Read the treasury design overview →

ECIP–1113 Olympia DAO Governance Framework

Specifies a modular DAO architecture that governs how the treasury authorizes disbursements. It separates core protocol funding from social choices around identity, delegation, and participation.

  • Defines roles, modules, and execution timelocks.
  • Supports Child-DAOs and specialized funding streams.
  • Does not hard-code voter identity or sybil resistance.

ECIP–1114 Funding Proposal Process

Standardizes the lifecycle of funding proposals—from submission and review to voting and milestone-based execution.

  • Defines proposal states, metadata, and execution rules.
  • Supports milestone-based payouts and escrows.
  • Integrates with both community-driven and institutional proposals.

ECIP–1115 Miner Incentive Smoothing (Optional)

Introduces an optional smoothing mechanism that redistributes BASEFEE over time, following a modeled “L-curve” to support a more stable security budget.

  • Helps miners plan around variable fee revenue.
  • Is explicitly optional and can be enabled or omitted by consensus.
  • Works alongside 1111–1114 without changing their core guarantees.

Modular by Design: 1111 can be adopted on its own to modernize fees and redirect BASEFEE. 1112–1114 add a treasury and governance layer. 1115 is optional and can be revisited independently as the network matures.

Design Principles

Olympia is intentionally aligned with Ethereum Classic’s long-standing philosophy. Each ECIP is written to respect a small set of non-negotiable constraints:

No monetary inflation Proof-of-Work preserved Immutability & “Code is Law” Transparent on-chain funding Modular governance Client diversity & compatibility

Rather than inventing a new “governance coin” or routing everything through a single foundation, Olympia:

  • Uses existing protocol revenue (BASEFEE) instead of new issuance.
  • Keeps the treasury contract immutable while allowing governance modules to evolve.
  • Separates capabilities (what the protocol can do) from social choices (who participates and how).
  • Maintains EVM compatibility by adopting 1559-style accounting without burning.

Questions like “Who gets to vote?”, “How are delegates chosen?”, or “Which identity system should be used?” are deliberately pushed to the social layer and future Olympia Improvement Proposals (OIPs), not enshrined in the base protocol.

Deployment Path & Phasing

Olympia follows the existing ECIP-1000 process and is expected to be rolled out in phases, with careful testing and community review at each step.

  1. Research & Historical Context
    Years of ETC community calls and research (2021–2024) documented long-term funding challenges, client fragility, and the need for protocol-native, non-inflationary treasury mechanisms.
  2. ECIP Drafting
    ECIPs 1111–1115 are drafted as a tightly-scoped, modular suite, each with a single, well-defined responsibility.
  3. Testnet Implementations
    Client and contract implementations are deployed on testnets (such as Mordor) to validate behavior, security assumptions, and operator experience.
  4. Community Review & Refinement
    Miners, node operators, developers, and ecosystem participants review the behavior, discuss tradeoffs, and suggest refinements via ECIP discussions and open calls.
  5. Mainnet Activation (Pending Consensus)
    If broad consensus forms, Olympia is scheduled for a future network upgrade following the established ETC governance process.
Specification-Driven Review: Decisions about Olympia are made through the ECIP-1000 process, with feedback grounded in the written specifications and observed behavior on testnets, rather than personalities or off-chain influence.
