ECIPs 1111–1115 · From Research to Deployment

A Phased Path for the Olympia Upgrade

Olympia is a multi-ECIP upgrade for Ethereum Classic that must be developed, tested, and adopted carefully. This roadmap summarizes how the suite—ECIPs 1111 through 1115—moves from research and drafting to testnet deployment, community review, and potential mainnet activation.

Exact dates and activation blocks are determined by the ECIP-1000 process and client readiness. The roadmap below is intentionally high-level and focuses on sequence and dependencies, not fixed timelines.

High-Level Phases

The Olympia roadmap can be thought of in five major phases. Some phases overlap in time, but each has distinct goals and outputs.

Phase 1 – Research & Problem Definition

Community calls, historical analysis, and design discussions identify ETC’s funding, security, and governance challenges. Existing fee models, treasury ideas, and DAO patterns are evaluated against Ethereum Classic’s values.

Phase 2 – ECIP Drafting & Review

ECIPs 1111–1115 are written as a modular suite: fee mechanics, treasury vault, governance framework, funding process, and optional smoothing. Drafts are refined through public review, GitHub feedback, and community calls.

Phase 3 – Testnet Implementations

Client and contract implementations are deployed on testnets (for example, Mordor). This phase validates consensus behavior, BASEFEE accounting, treasury logic, and DAO execution flows under real network conditions.

Phase 4 – Community Review & Consensus

Miners, node operators, developers, and ecosystem participants evaluate testnet results, discuss tradeoffs, and refine parameters. If broad consensus forms, ECIPs progress toward “Final” status per ECIP-1000.

Phase 5 – Mainnet Activation & Iteration

If adopted, Olympia is activated in a scheduled network upgrade. After launch, the DAO, treasury, and smoothing model can be tuned via future ECIPs and Olympia Improvement Proposals (OIPs), based on live data and community feedback.

Specification-Driven: At every phase, decisions are grounded in the written ECIPs, testnet behavior, and the ECIP-1000 process—not in private agreements or off-chain control.

Key Milestones & Dependencies

Olympia’s components are modular but interdependent. Some pieces can move ahead of others, while certain milestones must happen in order.

ECIP Suite Drafted (1111–1115)

Specification Complete · Subject to Ongoing Review

All five ECIPs exist as drafts that can be read, discussed, and refined. This provides a complete picture of fees, treasury, governance, funding, and smoothing—even if not all pieces are activated at once.

Fee & BASEFEE Implementation on Testnet (ECIP-1111)

Core Prerequisite for Treasury & Smoothing

EIP-1559-style BASEFEE accounting and type-2 transactions are implemented on a testnet. Clients are tested for correctness, performance, and compatibility with existing tooling.

Treasury Contract Deployment & Wiring (ECIP-1112)

Vault Ready · BASEFEE Destination Hook

The immutable treasury contract is deployed and wired to receive redirected BASEFEE on testnet. Explorers and dashboards begin tracking balances and flows.

DAO & Funding Flow on Testnet (ECIP-1113 & 1114)

Governance Loop Exercised End-to-End

Governance contracts, timelocks, and proposal flows are tested. Proposals are created, voted on, queued, and executed against the treasury on testnet to validate the full pipeline from idea to payment.

Optional Smoothing Modeling & Trials (ECIP-1115)

Security Budget & Miner Revenue Analysis

The L-curve smoothing model is simulated and, if desired, deployed on testnet. Miners and researchers analyze its impact on revenue stability and security over time.

Community Sign-Off & Client Readiness

ECIP-1000 · Multi-Stakeholder Consensus

If testnet results are satisfactory, the community converges on whether and how to activate Olympia on mainnet. Client teams coordinate releases and activation blocks.

Mainnet Activation (Pending Consensus)

Network Upgrade · Ongoing Monitoring

Olympia components agreed upon by the community are activated in a scheduled network upgrade. Post-activation, the system is closely monitored and future refinements are proposed through new ECIPs and OIPs.

Modular Dependencies: ECIP-1111 (fees) can, in principle, be adopted without the full treasury and DAO stack. ECIP-1112–1114 require 1111 to be meaningful. ECIP-1115 is optional and can be revisited independently as the network matures.

How the Community Can Participate

Olympia is designed to emerge from open collaboration between developers, miners, node operators, businesses, and community members. There are several ways to engage with the roadmap and influence outcomes.

Read & Review the ECIPs

Start with the written specifications. Provide feedback via GitHub comments, issues, or public forums. Concrete, line-specific suggestions are especially valuable.

Run Testnet Infrastructure

Node operators and miners can run testnet clients, help uncover issues, and contribute to performance and stability testing for ECIP-1111–1115.

Build Tools & Dashboards

Explorers, analytics, and governance dashboards make it easier for everyone to understand how Olympia works in practice—especially the treasury and DAO flows.

Propose Governance Configurations

Future Olympia Improvement Proposals (OIPs) can specify voter sets, delegate systems, or Child-DAO structures. Community members can draft and advocate for these designs.

Prepare Ecosystem Projects

Teams building clients, tools, dApps, or education programs can plan potential funding proposals under ECIP-1114, aligning their roadmaps with the Olympia treasury model.

Engage in Public Discussion

Community calls, forums, and social channels are where tradeoffs are debated and priorities are set. Clear, well-reasoned participation helps guide the roadmap toward outcomes that align with ETC’s core principles.

Participation, Not Permission: Olympia is architected so that anyone in the ETC ecosystem can contribute to research, testing, governance design, and proposal development. No single entity owns the roadmap.
