Roadmap · From Research to Mainnet

How Olympia Moves from Idea to Protocol Reality

Olympia is designed to follow Ethereum Classic’s existing governance process (ECIP-1000), not bypass it. The roadmap is structured into phases that reflect how protocol changes normally ship: research, specification, testnet implementation, review, and—if consensus forms—mainnet activation.

This page outlines those phases, key milestones, and where the Olympia ECIP suite (1111–1115) fits into each step.

Phases of the Olympia Roadmap

The roadmap is expressed as a set of broad phases rather than fixed calendar dates. This keeps the upgrade tied to quality and consensus instead of arbitrary deadlines.

Phase 1 · Research & Problem Definition

Phase 1 Funding Crisis & Design Space

Years of community calls and discussion clarified ETC’s funding problem and explored possible solutions:

  • Documenting the loss of external sponsorship and dormant community funds.
  • Analyzing tradeoffs between protocol treasuries, off-chain grants, and no funding.
  • Surveying EIP-1559, DAO frameworks, and treasury designs across the EVM ecosystem.

Outcome: consensus that a non-inflationary, protocol-native treasury is worth designing, if it can respect ETC’s values.

Phase 2 · Specification Drafting

Phase 2 ECIP Suite 1111–1115

The Olympia design was formalized into a modular ECIP suite:

  • 1111 – EVM & BASEFEE upgrades.
  • 1112 – Protocol treasury contract.
  • 1113 – Governance framework.
  • 1114 – Funding proposal process.
  • 1115 – Optional miner incentive smoothing.

Each ECIP is written to satisfy ECIP-1000 requirements: one idea per proposal, clear motivation, rationale, and specification sections.

Phase 3 · Testnet Implementations

Phase 3 Client & Contract Testing

In this phase, client and contract implementations of Olympia are deployed to ETC testnets (such as Mordor) to validate:

  • BASEFEE behavior and fee market dynamics (ECIP-1111).
  • Treasury invariants and interactions (ECIP-1112).
  • Governance module wiring and proposal flows (ECIP-1113/1114).
  • Optional smoothing behavior and parameters (ECIP-1115).

The goal is to uncover any bugs, edge cases, or UX issues before mainnet consideration.

Phase 4 · Community Review & Refinement

Phase 4 Broad Review & Parameter Tuning

Once testnet implementations are available, the ETC community—miners, node operators, developers, and users—engage in structured review:

  • Public calls and forums to discuss observed behavior and metrics.
  • Parameter tuning for BASEFEE, gas targets, and smoothing (if used).
  • Clarifications or amendments to ECIP text based on feedback.

This phase ends when there is either broad support to proceed or a clear need for additional iteration.

Phase 5 · Consensus & Mainnet Activation

Phase 5 Network Upgrade (If Approved)

If ETC stakeholders converge on support for Olympia, the suite is scheduled into a future network upgrade:

  • Clients implement a named hard fork including ECIP-1111–1114 (and optionally 1115).
  • Miners and node operators coordinate around the activation height.
  • Monitoring and incident response plans are prepared in advance.

If consensus does not form, Olympia can remain on testnet or be revised for a future cycle.

Process, Not Hype: The roadmap is meant to be realistic and aligned with ECIP-1000. Olympia advances only as fast as the quality of its implementations and the strength of consensus around it.

Key Milestones & Deliverables

Within each phase, there are concrete milestones that can be tracked and verified by the community. These help distinguish between “ideas on paper” and “code that runs”.

Spec ECIP Milestones

  • ECIPs 1111–1115 reach Draft status with full specifications.
  • Public review threads and issues tracked in the ECIP repository.
  • Converged spec for BASEFEE redirection semantics and treasury interface.

Deliverable: a stable set of ECIP documents suitable for implementation and testing.

Client Implementation Milestones

  • At least one ETC client with a complete ECIP-1111 implementation.
  • Client integration with the treasury address and BASEFEE redirect rules.
  • Testnet nodes and tooling (RPC, explorers) updated to understand Olympia fields.

Deliverable: a reference client ready for testnet activation of Olympia.

Contracts Treasury & DAO Milestones

  • Deployment of the ECIP-1112 treasury contract to testnet.
  • Deployment of ECIP-1113/1114 DAO modules and executors.
  • End-to-end flow: test proposals that move funds from treasury to recipients.

Deliverable: working on-chain governance stack exercising all core pathways.

Testing Observability & Metrics

  • Dashboards for BASEFEE behavior, treasury inflows, and DAO proposals.
  • Simulations of different usage patterns and gas parameters.
  • Reporting on miner, node operator, and user experience during testnet trials.

Deliverable: evidence-based reports to inform the community’s go/no-go decision.

Community Governance & Communication

  • Structured community calls focusing separately on fees, treasury, and governance.
  • Written explainers, FAQs, and educational content (like this site).
  • Clear summaries of objections, alternatives, and tradeoffs.

Deliverable: an informed community that understands what Olympia does and does not do.

Tracking Progress: Over time, this roadmap can be updated with concrete dates, links to PRs, testnet deployments, and call recordings so stakeholders can verify progress independently.

Relationship to ECIP-1000 & Governance

Olympia does not introduce a new governance process for protocol changes. It explicitly relies on ECIP-1000 for decisions about whether and when the suite should be activated on mainnet.

That means:

  • Each ECIP stands on its own and can be discussed or adopted independently.
  • Consensus is gauged through the usual channels: clients, miners, node operators, and community calls.
  • Protocol changes remain separate from treasury/DAO decisions.

Once deployed, Olympia’s DAO layer governs treasury outflows and public-goods funding, not protocol changes themselves. Future protocol-level changes continue to use the ECIP process.

Two Layers of Decision-Making: ECIP-1000 remains the mechanism for changing the protocol. Olympia introduces a separate, on-chain mechanism for allocating protocol funds within the existing consensus rules.

What’s Next for Olympia?

The next steps for Olympia depend on where ETC currently sits along this roadmap. In general:

  • If you are a client developer, focus on implementing and testing ECIP-1111 and treasury integration.
  • If you are a smart contract developer, help test the treasury and DAO contracts on testnet.
  • If you are a miner or node operator, participate in calls and trials to evaluate fee behavior and incentives.
  • If you are a community member or builder, engage with the educational material and provide feedback.

As more milestones are hit, this roadmap can expand into a living changelog linking to the actual code, deployments, and decisions that shape Olympia’s path.
