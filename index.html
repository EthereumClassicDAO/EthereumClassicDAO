Non-Inflationary Treasury & On-Chain Funding Layer for ETC

Olympia is a modular suite of Ethereum Classic Improvement Proposals (ECIPs 1111–1115) that introduces modern fee mechanics and a protocol-native, non-inflationary treasury. It adds a transparent, on-chain funding layer for public goods and ecosystem growth, while all protocol upgrades continue to follow ETC’s established ECIP-1000 process.

Olympia Overview

Ethereum Classic has historically relied on outside donors, grants, and volunteer work to fund essential development. Olympia introduces a protocol-native, transparent, non-inflationary funding layer by adding BASEFEE through ECIP-1111 and directing that revenue into an immutable on-chain treasury.

ECIP-1111 EVM & Fee Market Upgrades

Adds modern EVM features and introduces BASEFEE—an automatic fee component similar to EIP-1559. Instead of burning BASEFEE like Ethereum, ETC redirects it to a destination chosen by consensus, enabling a protocol-level funding stream.

ECIP-1112 Olympia Treasury Contract

Defines a permanent, protocol-controlled treasury that receives BASEFEE. The contract is immutable, cannot mint ETC, and can only release funds through approved governance actions, making the funding process auditable and predictable.

ECIP-1113 DAO Governance Framework

Establishes the modular governance system that authorizes treasury spending. It sets the rules for how proposals, voting modules, and execution work—without enforcing any specific voter identity or sybil-resistance model.

ECIP-1114 Funding Proposal Process

Standardizes how funding proposals move from idea to execution. Defines required metadata, review steps, voting periods, and milestone-based payouts, ensuring every request follows the same transparent process.

ECIP-1115 Miner Incentive Smoothing (Optional)

Adds an optional mechanism to smooth out BASEFEE revenue over time. This helps stabilize miner income during periods of fluctuating network usage, supporting long-term security planning.

Together, these ECIPs create a modular system that gives Ethereum Classic a reliable, transparent way to fund its ecosystem—without inflation, without trusted custodians, and without changing ECIP-1000 or core consensus rules.

ECIP Suite & Core Components

Olympia Treasury (ECIP-1112)

A permanent, protocol-controlled treasury that receives BASEFEE introduced by ECIP-1111. The contract cannot be upgraded, cannot mint ETC, and releases funds only through approved DAO actions—providing a transparent, non-inflationary source of public-goods funding.

Governance Framework (ECIP-1113 & 1114)

A modular DAO system that manages how treasury funds are requested, voted on, and executed. It defines proposal flows, voting and execution modules, and milestone-based payouts—while leaving voter identity and sybil-resistance models to the social layer and future OIPs.

Fees & Miner Incentives (ECIP-1111 & 1115)

ECIP-1111 introduces BASEFEE and modern EVM fee mechanics, improving predictability and compatibility with today’s tooling. ECIP-1115 adds an optional smoothing model that spreads BASEFEE revenue over time to help stabilize miner incentives during variable network usage.

About Ethereum Classic & Olympia

Ethereum Classic is the original Ethereum chain, grounded in the Code is Law philosophy, Proof-of-Work security, and a long-standing commitment to immutability, neutrality, and open participation.

Olympia builds on these principles without altering them. It introduces a transparent, on-chain funding layer for public goods, client maintenance, and ecosystem growth—while preserving ETC’s fixed monetary policy, consensus rules, and historical integrity. Olympia does not replace the ECIP-1000 process or change how protocol upgrades are made; it simply adds a sustainable way to fund the work that keeps the network healthy.

This site provides a high-level overview of the Olympia ECIPs. The full, canonical specifications are published in the official Ethereum Classic ECIP repository.
