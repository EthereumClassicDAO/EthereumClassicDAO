Non-Inflationary Treasury & On-Chain Funding Layer for ETC

Olympia is a set of Ethereum Classic Improvement Proposals (ECIPs 1111–1115). Together they add modern EIP-1559-style fee mechanics and a protocol-native, non-inflationary treasury. This creates a transparent on-chain funding layer for public goods and ecosystem growth, while all protocol upgrades continue to follow Ethereum Classic’s existing ECIP-1000 governance process.

Olympia Overview

Historically, Ethereum Classic has relied on outside donors, grants, and volunteer work to fund essential development. Olympia introduces a protocol-native, transparent, non-inflationary funding layer by adding BASEFEE—a protocol-level fee per unit of gas— through ECIP-1111 and redirecting that revenue into an immutable on-chain treasury.

ECIP-1111 EVM & Fee Market Upgrades

Adds modern EVM features and introduces BASEFEE, an automatic fee component similar to EIP-1559. Instead of burning BASEFEE like Ethereum, ETC redirects it to a destination chosen by consensus, enabling a protocol-level funding stream while keeping Proof-of-Work and fixed emission unchanged.

ECIP-1112 Olympia Treasury Contract

Defines a permanent, protocol-controlled treasury that receives redirected BASEFEE. The contract is immutable, cannot mint ETC, and can only release funds through approved governance actions, making the funding process auditable and predictable.

ECIP-1113 DAO Governance Framework

Establishes the modular governance system that authorizes treasury spending. It sets the rules for how proposals, voting modules, and execution work—without enforcing any specific voter identity or sybil-resistance model.

ECIP-1114 Funding Proposal Process

Standardizes how funding proposals move from idea to executed payment. It defines required metadata, review steps, voting periods, and milestone-based payouts so that every request follows the same transparent, on-chain process.

ECIP-1115 Miner Incentive Smoothing (Optional)

Adds an optional mechanism to smooth out BASEFEE revenue over time. This helps stabilize miner income during periods of fluctuating network usage and supports long-term security planning, without changing block rewards or emission.

Together, these ECIPs create a modular system that gives Ethereum Classic a reliable and transparent way to fund its ecosystem—without inflation, without trusted custodians, and without changing ECIP-1000 or core consensus rules.

ECIP Suite & Core Components

Olympia Treasury (ECIP-1112)

A permanent, protocol-controlled treasury that receives BASEFEE introduced by ECIP-1111. The contract cannot be upgraded, cannot mint ETC, and releases funds only through approved DAO actions—providing a transparent, non-inflationary source of public-goods funding.

Governance Framework (ECIP-1113 & 1114)

A modular DAO system that manages how treasury funds are requested, voted on, and executed. It defines proposal flows, voting and execution modules, and milestone-based payouts, while leaving voter identity and sybil-resistance models to the social layer and future Olympia Improvement Proposals (OIPs).

Fees & Miner Incentives (ECIP-1111 & 1115)

ECIP-1111 introduces BASEFEE and modern EVM fee mechanics, improving predictability and compatibility with today’s wallets and tooling. ECIP-1115 adds an optional smoothing model that spreads BASEFEE revenue over time to help stabilize miner incentives during variable network usage.

About Ethereum Classic & Olympia

Ethereum Classic is the original Ethereum chain, grounded in the Code is Law philosophy, Proof-of-Work security, and a long-standing commitment to immutability, neutrality, and open participation.

Olympia builds on these principles without altering them. It introduces a transparent, on-chain funding layer for public goods, client maintenance, and ecosystem growth—while preserving ETC’s fixed monetary policy, consensus rules, and historical integrity. Olympia does not change chain history, does not add inflation, and does not replace the ECIP-1000 process for protocol upgrades; it simply adds a sustainable way to fund the work that keeps the network healthy.

This site provides a high-level overview of the Olympia ECIPs. The full, canonical specifications are published in the official Ethereum Classic ECIP repository.
