Protocol-Native Treasury & Governance for ETC

Olympia is a proposed suite of Ethereum Classic Improvement Proposals (ECIPs 1111–1115) that introduces EIP-1559-style BASEFEE accounting, a non-inflationary protocol treasury, and a modular on-chain governance framework—all without changing ETC’s fixed-emission schedule or Proof-of-Work consensus.

Olympia Overview

For years, Ethereum Classic has relied on external donors and off-chain grants to fund core development and public goods. Olympia formalizes a protocol-native, transparent, and non-inflationary way for ETC to fund itself, using only existing fee revenue.

The Olympia ECIP suite comprises:

  • ECIP-1111 – Olympia EVM & Protocol Upgrades
    Introduces EIP-1559-style BASEFEE accounting and modern EVM features, and redirects BASEFEE to a consensus-chosen destination (such as the Olympia Treasury).
  • ECIP-1112 – Olympia Treasury Contract
    Defines an immutable, protocol-controlled treasury smart contract to receive redirected BASEFEE.
  • ECIP-1113 – Olympia DAO Governance Framework
    Specifies a modular DAO architecture for managing treasury disbursements, without hard-coding any specific voter identity or sybil-resistance scheme.
  • ECIP-1114 – Funding Proposal Process
    Standardizes how funding proposals are submitted, evaluated, voted on, and executed with milestone-based disbursements.
  • ECIP-1115 – Miner Incentive Smoothing (Optional)
    Introduces an optional smoothing model to redistribute BASEFEE over time, supporting a more stable long-term security budget.

Together, these ECIPs provide a cohesive, modular framework for sustainable development on Ethereum Classic.

ECIP Suite & Core Components

Olympia Treasury (ECIP-1112)

A protocol-controlled, immutable treasury smart contract that receives redirected BASEFEE from ECIP-1111. No new ETC is minted—funding comes entirely from existing protocol revenue.

Learn About the Treasury

Governance Framework (ECIP-1113 & 1114)

A modular DAO framework defining proposal modules, voting modules, and execution timelocks, plus a standardized funding proposal process. Voter identity and participation models remain a social-layer choice, not protocol-level law.

Explore Governance Design

Fees & Miner Incentives (ECIP-1111 & 1115)

EIP-1559-style BASEFEE accounting improves fee predictability and EVM compatibility, while the optional smoothing model in ECIP-1115 helps align long-term miner incentives with network usage.

About Ethereum Classic & Olympia

Ethereum Classic is the original Ethereum chain, preserving the Code is Law philosophy, Proof-of-Work security, and an uncompromising commitment to immutability and neutrality.

Olympia is designed to align with these principles. It does not introduce inflation, does not rewrite history, and does not centralize control. Instead, it offers a transparent, on-chain mechanism for funding public goods, maintaining clients, and growing the ETC ecosystem in a way that is accountable to the community.

This site provides a high-level view of the Olympia ECIPs. The canonical specifications live in the official Ethereum Classic ECIP repository.
