Vision · Ethereum Classic in the Next Decade

Programmable Proof-of-Work with Sustainable, Neutral Funding

Ethereum Classic aims to be the programmable base layer for Proof-of-Work assets: a credibly neutral settlement layer where smart contracts, hard money, and long-lived infrastructure can coexist without central gatekeepers.

Olympia fits into this vision as the funding engine behind ETC’s next decade: a way for the network to maintain itself, upgrade safely, and grow its ecosystem without giving up immutability, fixed supply, or Proof-of-Work.

Core Principles Strategic Frontiers

Core Principles for the Next Decade

Ethereum Classic’s long-term vision is grounded in a small set of principles that do not change, even as the ecosystem and tooling evolve.

Immutability & “Code is Law” Proof-of-Work security Fixed, predictable monetary policy Protocol neutrality & minimalism Open, permissionless participation Transparent, non-inflationary funding

Olympia is designed to extend these principles into the realm of funding and governance—without diluting them:

  • Non-inflationary treasury fed by BASEFEE, not new issuance.
  • Immutable vault (ECIP-1112) paired with evolvable governance modules (ECIP-1113/1114).
  • Clear separation between protocol rules and social choices about identity or voting.
Vision in one sentence: Ethereum Classic should remain the chain that never rewrites history, while becoming the chain that can reliably fund its own future.

ETC’s Role in the Broader Crypto Landscape

In a world of many chains, Ethereum Classic fills a specific niche:

  • Proof-of-Work smart contract platform: Combining PoW security with a fully programmable EVM execution environment.
  • Settlement layer for hard assets: A natural home for tokenized BTC, PoW assets, and long-lived financial contracts that value censorship resistance.
  • Neutral coordination layer: A base layer that does not privilege any single foundation, corporation, or governance token.

Olympia strengthens this role by:

  • Ensuring client diversity and maintenance can be funded long term.
  • Keeping core infrastructure and tooling healthy, even in bear markets.
  • Creating a public-goods magnet for ETC-aligned builders and institutions.

Strategic Frontiers for Ethereum Classic

Over the next 5–10 years, Olympia positions Ethereum Classic to pursue several strategic frontiers.

Frontier Sustainable Client Diversity

A resilient chain is not a single-client chain. With Olympia, ETC can:

  • Fund multiple independent clients with overlapping feature sets.
  • Invest in formal verification and testing across implementations.
  • Maintain client teams through full market cycles.

Long-term vision: at least two battle-tested, actively maintained clients at all times.

Frontier Core Infrastructure as a Public Good

Block explorers, RPC endpoints, testnets, and monitoring are not side projects—they are core public goods. Olympia provides:

  • Recurring, milestone-based budgets for critical services.
  • Incentives for redundancy and geographic distribution.
  • Clear expectations around uptime and observability.

Vision: an ETC ecosystem where no single company “owns” core infra, but everyone depends on a robust, shared base.

Frontier Programmable PoW & Cross-Asset Settlement

ETC’s EVM layer, combined with PoW security, makes it a strong candidate to host:

  • Wrapped PoW assets (e.g., BTC, LTC, other mined assets).
  • Cross-chain settlement for layer-2s and sidechains.
  • On-chain instruments tied to energy, hashpower, or compute markets.

Olympia can fund the bridges, primitives, and standards needed to make ETC a natural settlement hub for these systems.

Frontier Long-Horizon Public Goods & Research

Some work is inherently long-term: protocol research, education, security culture, documentation. Olympia enables:

  • Multi-year research initiatives aligned with ETC values.
  • Educational programs, docs, and translation efforts.
  • Security and governance research that benefits all ETC projects.

The treasury gives ETC a way to invest in things that don’t pay off in a single quarter.

Frontier Neutral Governance Experimentation

Because ECIP-1113/1114 are modular, ETC can host multiple governance experiments around a single treasury:

  • Identity-based systems and sybil-resistant participation models.
  • Delegate systems that balance expertise with broad input.
  • Hybrid approaches combining stake, reputation, and contribution.

The protocol remains neutral; experimentation lives in the DAO layer, not in consensus.

Frontier Institutional & Community Co-Funding

Olympia creates a neutral venue where:

  • Institutional partners can co-fund public goods alongside protocol revenue.
  • Community donations can be pooled and governed transparently.
  • Both can see exactly how funds are used on-chain.

Vision: ETC as a gathering point for long-term aligned capital, not just short-term speculation.

5–10 Year Scenarios

No roadmap is certain, but we can outline what “success” might look like if Olympia delivers on its design goals.

Scenario Resilient Base Layer

In a successful scenario:

  • ETC maintains multiple production-grade clients.
  • Core infrastructure is funded, redundant, and boringly reliable.
  • Protocol upgrades follow a predictable, well-documented process.

The chain becomes known for stability and professionalism, not drama.

Scenario Healthy Ecosystem Flywheel

With sustainable funding:

  • More builders deploy on ETC because they see long-term support.
  • More usage drives more BASEFEE and treasury inflow.
  • The DAO reinvests into clients, infra, and new projects.

Over time, ETC’s public-goods flywheel makes it increasingly attractive as a base layer.

Scenario Robust Governance Culture

A mature Olympia ecosystem develops:

  • Clear norms for evaluating proposals and tradeoffs.
  • Independent reviewers and watchdogs focused on treasury use.
  • Documented precedents that guide future decisions.

Governance becomes less about personalities and more about process and evidence.

Reality Check: These scenarios are not guaranteed. Olympia only provides the tools. The community’s choices over the next decade determine how powerful those tools become in practice.

What Olympia Does Not Change

A clear vision also requires clear boundaries. Olympia is not about changing:

  • Consensus: Ethereum Classic remains Proof-of-Work.
  • Monetary policy: No new issuance; the emission schedule stays fixed.
  • Chain history: No rewrites, no special bailouts.
  • Protocol governance: ECIP-1000 continues to govern protocol changes.

Instead, Olympia:

  • Adds capabilities for on-chain funding and governance.
  • Redirects an existing revenue stream (BASEFEE) into a shared treasury.
  • Creates structure around how the ecosystem invests in itself.

How Stakeholders Fit into the Vision

Turning this vision into reality requires sustained participation from many different types of stakeholders:

  • Client teams: Build, test, and maintain implementations of Olympia and future upgrades.
  • Infrastructure providers: Run the services that make ETC usable day to day.
  • Builders & researchers: Propose public-goods work aligned with ETC’s values.
  • Miners & node operators: Evaluate incentives, run upgraded clients, and provide feedback.
  • Users & holders: Track treasury flows, participate in governance, and hold decision-makers accountable.

Olympia’s design is meant to give each of these groups formal hooks into how ETC funds its future, rather than relying on informal influence or back-channel deals.
